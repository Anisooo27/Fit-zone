Advices

Start Slow: It's important to start slow and not push yourself too fast. This will prevent you from hurting yourself and getting discouraged.
Set clear goals: Set realistic and specific goals to help you stay motivated and measure your progress.
Follow a workout plan: Working with a personal trainer or following an online workout plan can help you learn basic moves and plan effective workouts.
Focus on form: Make sure you've mastered the basic moves before adding weight or intensity. Form is key to avoiding injury and getting the most out of each exercise.
Stay hydrated and well fueled: Drink enough water before, during and after training, and eat healthy foods to support your body during exercise.
Be regular: try to train regularly, even if it's only a few times a week. Consistency is the key to long-term success.
Listen to your body: If you feel any pain or discomfort, stop immediately and see a healthcare professional. It is important to take care of your body to avoid injuries.
Avoid overestimating your abilities: it is important to start slowly and not push your body beyond its limits. Listen to your body and progress at your own pace.
Avoid neglecting the warm-up: a good warm-up is essential to prepare your body for physical exertion and to avoid injuries. Never skip the warm-up.
Avoid resting too long: It's important to rest between sets of exercises, but don't take too long. This can slow your heart rate and blood circulation, which can affect your performance.
Avoid neglecting stretching: Stretching helps prevent injury and improve your flexibility. Don't forget to stretch after training.
Avoid comparing yourself to others: everyone is different, with different goals and abilities. Don't compare yourself to others and focus on your own progress.
Avoid skipping training days: consistency is important for getting results. Try to train regularly, even if you can't train every day.
Don't neglect nutrition: your diet plays an important role in your fitness success. Make sure you eat enough protein, vegetables and fruit to support your body during exercise.
Eat enough protein: Protein is important for muscle growth and post-workout recovery. Aim to consume between 1.2 and 1.7 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight each day.
Eat complex carbohydrates: Complex carbohydrates provide energy for your workout and help maintain your energy level during exercise. Foods high in complex carbohydrates include whole grains, sweet potatoes, and green vegetables.
Eat healthy fats: Healthy fats are important for overall health and can help reduce inflammation in the body. Sources of healthy fats include nuts, avocados, and vegetable oils like olive oil and coconut oil.
Drink enough water: It is important to stay hydrated during training to maintain your energy level and improve your performance. Drink enough water before, during and after training.
Avoid processed foods: Processed foods can contain additives and preservatives that can harm your overall health. Try to eat fresh, unprocessed foods as much as possible.
Avoid foods high in added sugars: Foods high in added sugars can cause blood sugar spikes and make you feel tired during a workout. Try to limit your intake of added sugar and focus on whole, nutritious foods.
Vegetable Omelet: An omelet with vegetables like spinach, mushrooms, peppers, and cheese can provide a good amount of protein, calories, and nutrients.
Grilled Chicken with Grilled Vegetables: Grilled chicken breast with grilled vegetables like asparagus, zucchini, and bell peppers can be a rich source of protein, fiber, and nutrients.
Protein smoothie: A protein smoothie with milk or plant-based milk, fruits, vegetables, protein powder, and peanut butter or nuts can be a high-calorie, high-protein snack.
Steak and Roasted Sweet Potatoes: Grilled steak with roasted sweet potatoes can provide a good amount of protein, complex carbs, and nutrients.
Chili con carne: Chili con carne made with ground beef, beans, vegetables, and spices can be a rich source of protein, fiber, and complex carbohydrates.
Rolled oats with dried fruits and nuts: rolled oats cooked with milk or vegetable milk, dried fruits such as raisins or dates and nuts such as almonds, cashews or Brazil nuts can be a rich source of calories, protein and fibre.
Warm up: Before you start your workout, take the time to warm up to avoid injury. A warm-up can include dynamic stretching, light cardiovascular exercise, or specific movements for the muscles you'll be working.
Gradual Progression: Don't rush to increase the weight or intensity of your workout. Be sure to progress gradually and give your body time to adjust to the workout.
Correct Technique: Learn the correct techniques for each exercise and make sure you perform them correctly to avoid injury and maximize results.
Hydration: Make sure you drink enough water before, during and after training to maintain your hydration level and improve your performance.
Rest and Recovery: Be sure to allow plenty of time for recovery after each workout. Sufficient sleep, stretching, proper nutrition, and active recovery (such as light walks or stretching) can help improve recovery and prevent injury.
Variety: Don't be afraid to vary your workout routine to avoid monotony and avoid progression plateaus. Try changing up your program every few weeks to continue challenging your body and improving your results.
Tracking: Keep a log of your progress, including weights, reps, rest times, etc. This can help you track your progress, identify weak spots, and adjust your training program accordingly.
Be creative: Vary exercises and training programs to avoid monotony and keep your clients motivated.
Stay Informed: Stay up to date on the latest fitness trends and techniques by reading articles, taking training courses, and attending conferences.
Listen to your clients: Listen carefully to your clients' needs and goals, and adapt your training program accordingly. Be available to answer their questions and concerns.
Encourage and Motivate: Encourage your customers to achieve their goals by motivating them and providing them with positive feedback. Celebrate their successes with them.
Take care of your health: Take care of your own health and well-being by following a regular exercise program, eating a healthy diet, and getting enough rest and recovery.
Stay humble: Remember that you are there to help others achieve their fitness goals, not to show off. Stay humble and focus on your customers' needs.
Grow your business: Grow your business by using social media, expanding your customer base, and creating special programs for special events or special groups.
