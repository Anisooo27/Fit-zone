UpperBody

Chest:

Barbell Bench Press:

Barbell bench press: This is a classic exercise that targets your entire chest, as well as your triceps and shoulders. Lie on a flat bench with your feet flat on the ground and grip the barbell with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Lower the bar to your chest, pause briefly, and then press it back up to the starting position.

Dumbell Bench Press:

Dumbbell bench press: This exercise is similar to the barbell bench press, but it allows for a greater range of motion and can help you work each side of your chest independently. Lie on a flat bench with your feet flat on the ground and hold a dumbbell in each hand. Lower the dumbbells to your chest, pause briefly, and then press them back up to the starting position.

Cable Crossover:

Cable crossover: This exercise targets the middle and outer part of your chest. Stand in the middle of a cable machine with the cables set to the highest position. Grab each cable with one hand and step forward, so your arms are extended out to your sides. Bring your hands together in front of your chest, pause briefly, and then slowly release back to the starting position.

Push Ups:

Push-ups: This is a simple bodyweight exercise that can be done anywhere, and it targets your entire chest, as well as your triceps and shoulders. Start in a plank position with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Lower your body until your chest touches the ground, pause briefly, and then push back up to the starting position.

Biceps:

Barbell Curls:

Barbell curls: This classic exercise is one of the best for building overall mass in the biceps. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a barbell with an underhand grip. Keep your elbows close to your sides and curl the bar up towards your chest, then slowly lower it back down. Repeat for several sets of 8-12 reps.

Hammer Curls:

Hammer curls: This exercise targets the brachialis muscle in the biceps, which can help give your arms a more defined look. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold dumbbells with a neutral grip (palms facing in). Curl the dumbbells up towards your shoulders, then slowly lower them back down. Repeat for several sets of 8-12 reps.

Cable Curls:

Cable curls: This exercise can provide a different type of resistance than free weights and can help improve overall muscle definition. Stand facing a cable machine with a straight bar attachment. Grip the bar with an underhand grip and curl it up towards your chest, then slowly lower it back down. Repeat for several sets of 8-12 reps.

Concentration Curls:

Concentration curls: This exercise isolates the biceps and can help improve overall arm strength. Sit on a bench with your feet flat on the floor and hold a dumbbell in one hand. Rest your elbow on the inside of your thigh and curl the dumbbell up towards your shoulder, then slowly lower it back down. Repeat for several sets of 8-12 reps on each arm.

Triceps:

Tricep PushDowns:

Tricep pushdowns: This exercise targets the triceps and can help improve overall arm strength. Stand facing a cable machine with a rope attachment. Grip the rope with both hands and keep your elbows close to your sides. Push the rope down towards your thighs, then slowly bring it back up. Repeat for several sets of 8-12 reps.

Close Grip Bench Press:

Close-grip bench press: This exercise targets the triceps and chest muscles. Lie down on a bench with a barbell, using a close grip (hands shoulder-width apart). Lower the barbell towards your chest, then push it back up to the starting position.
Repeat for several sets of 8-12 reps.

overhead Tricep Extensions:

Overhead tricep extensions: This exercise targets the triceps and can help improve overall arm strength. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell with both hands. Lift the dumbbell over your head and lower it behind your head, keeping your elbows close to your ears. Then, lift the dumbbell back up to the starting .Repeat for several sets of 8-12 reps.

Dips:

Dips: This exercise targets the triceps, chest, and shoulders. Find a set of parallel bars and stand between them. Place your hands on the bars and lift yourself up, straightening your arms. Lower yourself down slowly, bending your arms at the elbows, then push yourself back up to the starting position. Repeat for several sets of 8-12 reps.

Back:

Lat Pulldowns:

Lat pulldowns: This exercise targets the latissimus dorsi muscles in the back and can help improve overall back strength. Sit at a lat pulldown machine and grip the bar with an overhand grip. Pull the bar down towards your chest, then slowly release it back up to the starting position. Repeat for several sets of 8-12 reps.

One Arm Barbell rows:

Barbell rows: This exercise targets the muscles in the upper and middle back, as well as the biceps. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a barbell with an overhand grip. Lean forward slightly and lift the barbell up towards your chest, keeping your elbows close to your sides. Lower the barbell back down to the starting position. Repeat for several sets of 8-12 reps.

Pull Ups:

Pull-ups: This exercise targets the latissimus dorsi muscles and can help improve overall upper body strength. Find a pull-up bar and grip it with an overhand grip. Hang from the bar and lift yourself up until your chin is above the bar, then slowly lower yourself back down. Repeat for several sets of 8-12 reps.

Deadlifts:

Deadlifts: This exercise targets the muscles in the lower back, as well as the hamstrings, glutes, and core. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a barbell with an overhand grip. Lift the barbell up off the ground, keeping your back straight and your core tight. Lower the barbell back down to the starting position. Repeat for several sets of 8-12 reps. Note: this exercise requires proper form and should be approached with caution if you're new to weightlifting.

Abdominals:

Crunches:

Crunches: This exercise targets the rectus abdominis muscles in the front of the abdomen. Lie on your back with your knees bent and your hands behind your head. Curl your shoulders up towards your knees, keeping your lower back on the floor. Lower yourself back down to the starting position. Repeat for several sets of 8-12 reps.

Planks

Planks: This exercise targets the transverse abdominis muscles, which are located deep in the abdomen and help support the lower back. Get into a push-up position, but instead of lowering yourself down, hold your body in a straight line, resting on your forearms and toes. Hold this position for several sets of 30-60 seconds.

Russian Twists:

Russian twists: This exercise targets the oblique muscles on the sides of the abdomen. Sit on the floor with your knees bent and your feet flat on the ground. Lean back slightly and lift your feet off the ground, balancing on your sit bones. Hold a weight or medicine ball with both hands and twist your torso to the right, then to the left. Repeat for several sets of 8-12 reps.

Cable Rotations:

Cable rotations: This exercise targets the oblique muscles on the sides of the abdomen. Stand facing a cable machine with a handle attachment. Hold the handle with both hands and twist your torso away from the machine, then slowly bring it back to the starting position. Repeat for several sets of 8-12 reps on each side.

LowerBody

Legs:

Leg press:

Sit on a leg press machine with your back against the backrest and your feet flat on the footplate. Push the footplate away from you until your legs are straight, then bend your knees to lower the weight back down. Repeat for the desired number of reps, being careful not to let your knees bend too far inward or outward.

Leg extensions:

Leg extensions are a popular gym exercise that focus on strengthening the muscles in the front of your thighs, specifically the quadriceps.It's important to use a weight that is challenging but manageable for you, and to avoid locking your knees or using momentum to lift the weight. Start with a lighter weight and focus on good form, gradually increasing the weight as you get stronger. You can also do single-leg extensions, lifting one leg at a time, to add more challenge and balance work.

Calves:

Standing calf raises:

Stand with the balls of your feet on a raised platform, such as a step or calf raise machine. Hold onto a support for balance if needed. Lower your heels down as far as you can, then lift up onto the balls of your feet, squeezing your calves at the top of the movement. Lower back down and repeat for the desired number of reps.

Donkey calf raises:

Stand with your toes on a block or step and lean forward, placing your hands on a bench or support for balance. Have a partner or trainer sit on your lower back, or use a calf raise machine that allows for this variation. Lower your heels down as far as you can, then lift up onto the balls of your feet, squeezing your calves at the top of the movement. Lower back down and repeat for the desired number of reps.
